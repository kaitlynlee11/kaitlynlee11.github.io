Some stuff about me:

I graduated from University of California, Berkeley in Spring 2024 with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering and a minor in Data Science. I've been doing robotics since high school-- whether it be manufacturing parts for FRC or designing armor for my combat robotics team. Robotics and controls has been a constant in my life as reflected in my coursework and projects. In addition, I've interned at Blue Robotics--an underwater ROV company--and Zebra Technologies--specifically for their warehouse robot acquisition Fetch Robotics. While at UC Berkeley, I was also in Tau Beta Pi, the engineering honors society, as well as Pi Tau Sigma, the mechanical engineering honors society.

Throughout my coursework, I've worked in teams to build machines that could do things like climb stairs, play chess, and self balance. I've also conducted, written, and presented research papers for materials and modeling projects for classes.

I'm passionate about using robotics and automation to solve interesting day-to-day problems and simplify difficult manufacturing procedures. In my free time, I love drawing, bouldering, and cooking!

Soft Gripper Modeling

Attempted to derive a new way to mathematically model soft fingered grippers using a mix of Bezier and Hermite curves. I worked on the data processing to fit the curve models to the hardware using MATLAB's image processing package and Python. The paper can be read below.

Mini Acrobat

Derived and simulated the acrobat model in MATLAB and prototyped an automated 3DOF acrobat with hardware. The physical model was created using 3D printed parts, servo motors, and timing belts. I worked on the CAD model as well as the manufacturing and assembly of the final product. More can be read below.

ChessBot

Created an automated chess board that magnetically moves pieces via gantry, using Stockfish AI to make opponent moves. I worked on creating and building the gantry to magnetically move the chess pieces. Limit and reed switches were used for sensing. Laser cut wood and acrylic sheets, 3D printed attachment pieces, 8020 bars were used to assemble the final product.

Comparing 3D Printed PLA vs. Casted PLA

Used an Instron Tensile Testing machine to judge the tensile strength various PLA specimens using casting and multiple 3D printing settings. Two types of 3D printer settings were used and two different home casting methods were used to manufacture the PLA. More can be read in the paper below.

Testing the Validity of Tegris in Combat Robotics Applications

Created an impromptu drop testing machine to determine the validity of the composite material, Tegris, in combat robotics competitions of 15lb robots. The Tegris was unsuccessful in receiving hits from the drop test machine designed for 15lb robots up until 7.5mm sheets. More can be read about it in the paper below.

Stair-Climbing Robot

Successfully created a robot using VEX parts to climb stairs using planar mechanisms. For the project, I 3D modeled and animated the robot in Solidworks.
